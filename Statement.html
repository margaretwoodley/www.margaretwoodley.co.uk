Artist’s Statement

My work deals with natural desires that are often repressed within us and what happens to those desires when they are blocked.  The resulting  anger that we all  experience is symbolized in my work through sexuality. This frustration and, at times, aggression, can be directed both inwardly at ourselves, and outwardly at those around us, society's controlling norms and  restrictive expectations. It's as if we are driven to feel abnormal when we are just trying to be ourselves. We can also have feelings of shame symbolized and expressed through our sexual desires and sexuality. These contradictions between our desires, restrictions and anger are represented in many of my images.

My style could be viewed as eroticism or pornography. It could also be seen as derived from or aimed at Feminist, Lesbian or Gay communities. Although relevant to these groups, my work relates to the whole of society. It results from my personal theological and spiritual journey as well as my understanding of Freud, Jung and D H Lawrence. I see connections in my work with artist such as Dali, Picasso, Duchamp and Louise Bourgeois. Other areas of my work, not directly connected to sexuality, deal with internal difficulties in navigating our path through life which we neither understand nor agree with. My aim is to use my work to raise these issues for both the individual and society.
